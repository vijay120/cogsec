# CBT Journal Template

## Why This Is Different From Regular Journaling

Traditional journaling has no structure and no feedback loop. You write, it disappears into a file, nothing changes. That's why it felt like a dump.

CBT journaling is different:

1. **It's structured** — you fill in specific columns, not free-write
2. **It's short** — one entry takes 3-5 minutes, not 20
3. **It has a built-in insight** — the "balanced thought" column IS the payoff. You don't need to review later.
4. **The benefit is in the writing, not the reading** — the cognitive reappraisal happens as you write. Re-reading is optional.

You don't need to review these entries. The act of writing them rewires the thought pattern in real time. If you never look at them again, they still worked.

That said, weekly review of patterns (not individual entries) is valuable. See the weekly review template for how.

## The 4-Column Entry

Fill this out once per day, or whenever something bothers you.

### Entry Date: _______________

**1. Situation** (facts only — what a camera would record)

> _______________________________________________________________
> _______________________________________________________________

**2. Automatic Thought** (what went through your mind — the raw, unfiltered reaction)

> _______________________________________________________________
> _______________________________________________________________

**3. Distortion Type** (check which apply)

- [ ] Catastrophizing — "This is a disaster"
- [ ] All-or-nothing — "I completely failed"
- [ ] Mind reading — "They think I'm..."
- [ ] Overgeneralization — "This always happens"
- [ ] Emotional reasoning — "I feel X, therefore X is true"
- [ ] Should statements — "I should be..."
- [ ] Discounting positives — "That doesn't count because..."
- [ ] Personalization — "This is my fault" (when it isn't)
- [ ] Fortune telling — "This will definitely go wrong"
- [ ] Labeling — "I'm a failure" (instead of "this attempt failed")

**4. Balanced Thought** (not positive spin — accurate reassessment)

> _______________________________________________________________
> _______________________________________________________________

**5. Outcome** (optional — how do you feel after reappraising? 1-10)

Before reappraisal: ___/10
After reappraisal: ___/10

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Work situation

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Situation | Colleague didn't respond to my Slack message for 4 hours |
| Automatic thought | "They're ignoring me. They don't think my work is important." |
| Distortion | Mind reading + Personalization |
| Balanced thought | "They're probably busy. A 4-hour delay on Slack is normal. If it were urgent I'd call. Nothing here indicates disrespect." |

### Example 2: Social situation

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Situation | Went to a meetup, had surface-level conversations, left early |
| Automatic thought | "I'll never find my people here. Moving was a mistake." |
| Distortion | Fortune telling + All-or-nothing + Overgeneralization |
| Balanced thought | "One meetup is one data point. Surface-level conversation is normal at first meetings. It took years to build my previous network. I need more attempts, not a verdict." |

### Example 3: Self-judgment

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Situation | Browsed social media for 45 minutes instead of doing deep work |
| Automatic thought | "I have no discipline. I'll never stick to this system." |
| Distortion | Labeling + Fortune telling + All-or-nothing |
| Balanced thought | "I slipped once. That's information, not identity. What was the trigger? I was tired and didn't take a break after the last focus block. Next time: mandatory break after 90 min." |

## Pattern Tracking (Weekly)

At your weekly review, scan this week's entries and note:

1. **Most common distortion type:** _______________
   (This is your cognitive blind spot — the bias your brain defaults to)

2. **Most common trigger situation:** _______________
   (This is where your defenses are weakest)

3. **Did reappraisals feel accurate?** Yes / Somewhat / Forced
   (If forced, the balanced thoughts might still be too "positive spin" — aim for truly accurate, even if uncomfortable)
