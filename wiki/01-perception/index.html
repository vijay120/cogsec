    Layer 1: Perception — What Gets In¶

    Your cognitive security starts with what you allow into your mind. Garbage in, garbage out applies to thoughts as much as code.

    Sleep (Foundation — Non-Negotiable)¶

    Sleep is not a Layer 1 practice — it's the foundation all three layers sit on.

    What happens without it:

    • Emotional regulation collapses (amygdala reactivity increases ~60%)
    • You can't distinguish real threats from noise
    • Cognitive bias sensitivity spikes
    • Willpower and executive function degrade
    • Memory consolidation fails

    The protocol:

    • 7-9 hours, consistent schedule (same wake time even on weekends)
    • Dark, cool room (18-20°C / 65-68°F)
    • No screens 30-60 min before bed (blue light suppresses melatonin)
    • No caffeine after 2pm (caffeine half-life is 5-6 hours)
    • If you wake up and can't sleep after 20 min, get up and do something boring in dim light, then return

    Key research: Walker, "Why We Sleep" (2017); Krause et al. (2017) on sleep deprivation and emotional brain reactivity.

    Priority: Do this first. Everything else degrades without it.

    Exercise (Infrastructure)¶

    Not a nice-to-have. Effect size on mood rivals SSRIs in clinical trials.

    What it does:

    • BDNF release — neurogenesis (literally grows new brain cells)
    • Cortisol regulation — better stress response
    • Improved executive function for 2-4 hours post-exercise
    • Sleep quality improvement (reinforcing cycle)

    The protocol:

    • 30 min moderate intensity, 4-5x/week
    • "Moderate" = can talk but not sing
    • Morning exercise is optimal for mood + cognitive boost throughout day
    • Consistency matters more than intensity

    Key research: Blumenthal et al. (2007) — exercise vs. sertraline for depression; Ratey, "Spark" (2008).

    Information Diet¶

    The principle: Treat information sources like dependencies — audit regularly, remove what's unused or harmful.

    Monthly curation sweep:

    • Unfollow accounts that trigger reactive emotions without teaching you anything
    • Mute or block without guilt — this is hygiene, not rudeness
    • Replace doom-scroll sources with one high-quality long-form source per interest area

    Morning no-input window:

    • First 30-60 min after waking: no phone, no email, no news
    • Your first thoughts set the cognitive frame for the day
    • Other people's priorities will hijack this window if you let them

    Emotional flag protocol:

    • When content makes you angry, anxious, or fearful: pause 10 seconds
    • Name the emotion: "I notice I'm feeling angry"
    • This activates prefrontal cortex and dampens amygdala response
    • Then decide: is this worth my cognitive resources?

    Key research: Lieberman et al. (2007) — affect labeling reduces amygdala activation; Newport, "Digital Minimalism" (2019).

    How These Compound¶

    Good sleep --> better emotional regulation --> better information filtering
     |
Exercise --> better mood baseline --> less vulnerability to manipulation
     |
Clean inputs --> clearer thinking --> better decisions --> more energy for sleep/exercise

    Each practice reinforces the others. Start with sleep, add exercise, then refine your information diet.
