# Layer 1: Perception — What Gets In

Your cognitive security starts with what you allow into your mind. Garbage in, garbage out applies to thoughts as much as code.

## Sleep (Foundation — Non-Negotiable)

Sleep is not a Layer 1 practice — it's the foundation all three layers sit on.

**What happens without it:**

- Emotional regulation collapses (amygdala reactivity increases ~60%)
- You can't distinguish real threats from noise
- Cognitive bias sensitivity spikes
- Willpower and executive function degrade
- Memory consolidation fails

**The protocol:**

- 7-9 hours, consistent schedule (same wake time even on weekends)
- Dark, cool room (18-20°C / 65-68°F)
- No screens 30-60 min before bed (blue light suppresses melatonin)
- No caffeine after 2pm (caffeine half-life is 5-6 hours)
- If you wake up and can't sleep after 20 min, get up and do something boring in dim light, then return

**Key research:** Walker, "Why We Sleep" (2017); Krause et al. (2017) on sleep deprivation and emotional brain reactivity.

**Priority: Do this first. Everything else degrades without it.**

### CBT for Insomnia (CBT-I)

CBT-I is the **first-line clinical treatment** for insomnia — recommended over medication by both AASM and ACP guidelines. Effects are durable at 12-month follow-up. Sleep medication loses efficacy over time; CBT-I gets stronger.

**The 5 components:**

#### 1. Sleep Restriction (most powerful component)

This is counterintuitive and uncomfortable, but it's the active ingredient.

- Calculate your actual average sleep time (e.g., 5.5 hours)
- Set a fixed wake time (e.g., 6:00 AM) — non-negotiable, even weekends
- Only allow yourself in bed for that window (e.g., 12:30 AM – 6:00 AM)
- Track sleep efficiency: (time asleep / time in bed) x 100
- When efficiency hits 85%+, expand the window by 15 minutes
- Repeat until you reach your target

The first 1-2 weeks feel terrible. That's the point — you're building sleep drive and consolidating fragmented sleep. It works.

#### 2. Stimulus Control

The goal: your brain should associate bed with sleep, not with lying awake worrying.

- Bed is for sleep only (no phone, no reading, no TV)
- Go to bed only when sleepy (not just tired)
- If awake for more than 15-20 minutes, get up
- Go to another room, do something boring in dim light
- Return only when sleepy again
- No clock-watching (turn clocks away)

#### 3. Cognitive Restructuring (CBT for sleep-specific thoughts)

Same technique as the CBT journal, but targeting sleep catastrophizing:

| Automatic thought | Balanced thought |
|---|---|
| "I won't function tomorrow" | "I've had bad nights before and still managed" |
| "I need exactly 8 hours" | "Sleep needs vary. Some nights less is fine." |
| "My insomnia is permanent" | "CBT-I works for most people in 3-4 weeks" |
| "If I don't sleep now I'll ruin tomorrow" | "One bad night doesn't ruin a day. The pressure to sleep is making it harder." |

#### 4. Sleep Hygiene (necessary but insufficient alone)

- Consistent wake time (the single most important habit)
- Cool room (18-20°C / 65-68°F), dark, quiet
- No caffeine 10+ hours before bed (not just after 2pm — adjust to your sensitivity)
- No alcohol — it helps onset but destroys REM architecture and causes early waking
- No screens 30-60 min before bed

#### 5. Relaxation Training

Pick one and practice consistently, not just on bad nights:

- **Progressive muscle relaxation:** Tense each muscle group 5 seconds, release 10 seconds, work from toes to head
- **Diaphragmatic breathing:** 4 seconds in (belly rises), 7 seconds out. 5-10 minutes.
- **Body scan:** Attention moves slowly from feet to head, noticing sensation without changing anything
- **Yoga Nidra:** Guided "yogic sleep" practice. Limited but promising evidence. Works as a relaxation technique. 10-20 min recordings available on YouTube and Insight Timer. Good complement, not a replacement for sleep restriction.

**CBT-I program structure:** Typically 6-8 weeks. Effects appear by weeks 3-4. Can be self-guided.

**Self-guided CBT-I resources:**

- **Insomnia Coach** (VA app, free) — self-guided 5-week CBT-I program
- **CBTI Coach** (VA app, free) — companion app for tracking
- **"Say Good Night to Insomnia"** by Gregg Jacobs — book-based CBT-I
- **"The Sleep Solution"** by W. Chris Winter — practical and accessible

**Key research:** Trauer et al. (2015, *Annals of Internal Medicine*) — meta-analysis showing effect sizes d=0.8-1.0 for sleep onset latency. Comparable short-term to sleep medication, superior long-term.

### Sleep Supplements (What Actually Has Evidence)

**Magnesium glycinate (recommended form):**

- 200-400mg elemental magnesium before bed
- Glycinate form is best absorbed and the glycine itself has calming properties
- Citrate is acceptable but more laxative effect
- Oxide is poorly absorbed — skip it
- Many people are mildly deficient, especially with modern diets

**Glycine:**

- 3g before bed
- Modest evidence for subjective sleep quality and next-day alertness
- Key research: Yamadera et al. (2007); Bannai et al. (2012)

**L-theanine:**

- 200mg
- Reduces anxiety, may improve sleep quality indirectly
- Evidence is modest but low risk

**What doesn't work:**

- **Melatonin for insomnia** — effective for jet lag and circadian disorders, weak for primary insomnia. Most pills (5-10mg) are pharmacological doses. Physiological dose is 0.3-0.5mg.
- **Antihistamines (Benadryl/ZzzQuil)** — tolerance develops within days, anticholinergic risks long-term
- **Alcohol** — worst sleep "aid." Destroys REM sleep.
- **Staying in bed longer trying to sleep** — actively makes insomnia worse (opposite of sleep restriction)
- **Sleep hygiene alone** — necessary foundation but insufficient without the behavioral components (sleep restriction + stimulus control)

## Exercise (Infrastructure)

Not a nice-to-have. Effect size on mood rivals SSRIs in clinical trials.

**What it does:**

- BDNF release — neurogenesis (literally grows new brain cells)
- Cortisol regulation — better stress response
- Improved executive function for 2-4 hours post-exercise
- Sleep quality improvement (reinforcing cycle)

**The protocol:**

- 30 min moderate intensity, 4-5x/week
- "Moderate" = can talk but not sing
- Morning exercise is optimal for mood + cognitive boost throughout day
- Consistency matters more than intensity

**Key research:** Blumenthal et al. (2007) — exercise vs. sertraline for depression; Ratey, "Spark" (2008).

## Information Diet

**The principle:** Treat information sources like dependencies — audit regularly, remove what's unused or harmful.

**Monthly curation sweep:**

- Unfollow accounts that trigger reactive emotions without teaching you anything
- Mute or block without guilt — this is hygiene, not rudeness
- Replace doom-scroll sources with one high-quality long-form source per interest area

**Morning no-input window:**

- First 30-60 min after waking: no phone, no email, no news
- Your first thoughts set the cognitive frame for the day
- Other people's priorities will hijack this window if you let them

**Emotional flag protocol:**

- When content makes you angry, anxious, or fearful: pause 10 seconds
- Name the emotion: "I notice I'm feeling angry"
- This activates prefrontal cortex and dampens amygdala response
- Then decide: is this worth my cognitive resources?

**Key research:** Lieberman et al. (2007) — affect labeling reduces amygdala activation; Newport, "Digital Minimalism" (2019).

## How These Compound

```
Good sleep --> better emotional regulation --> better information filtering
     |
Exercise --> better mood baseline --> less vulnerability to manipulation
     |
Clean inputs --> clearer thinking --> better decisions --> more energy for sleep/exercise
```

Each practice reinforces the others. Start with sleep, add exercise, then refine your information diet.
